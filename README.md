# Impact of Maternal Smoking on Low Birth Weight

## Overview

This study examines the association between maternal smoking during pregnancy and low birth weight (LBW) using retrospective clinical data from Baystate Medical Center.
Low birth weight was defined as birth weight < 2500 grams.

## Study Design
- Observational retrospective study  
- Sample size: n = 189 births  
- Setting: Baystate Medical Center  

## Methods
- Descriptive statistics  
- Chi-square and Fisher’s exact tests  
- Unadjusted logistic regression  
- Multivariable logistic regression  
## Key Findings
Maternal smoking significantly increased the odds of low birth weight.
Adjusted Odds Ratio (AOR): 3.03  
95% CI: 1.44 – 6.37  
p-value: 0.0035  
Race and hypertension were also significant predictors.
## Software
SAS 9.4

## Data Availability
The dataset consists of clinical data from Baystate Medical Center.
Due to patient privacy protections and institutional data restrictions, the dataset is not publicly available and is not included in this repository.
